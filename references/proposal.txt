Project Proposal
Volatility-Managed Portfolio Allocation under Tail Risk Constraints
A GARCH-Informed CVaR Optimization Strategy
1. Abstract

Traditional portfolio allocation strategies such as the 60/40 stock-bond portfolio are simple and widely used, but they often fail to adapt to changing market conditions and may suffer large drawdowns during periods of market stress. In this project, we propose a dynamic asset allocation strategy that combines volatility forecasting and downside risk optimization.

Specifically, we use GARCH-based volatility forecasting to estimate future market risk and apply Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) optimization to construct portfolios that explicitly control tail risk. The strategy dynamically rebalances between equity and bond assets based on updated market conditions.

We evaluate the proposed strategy using historical market data and compare it against traditional benchmark portfolios such as static 60/40 allocation and buy-and-hold strategies. Our goal is to investigate whether volatility-informed tail-risk optimization can improve risk-adjusted portfolio performance.

2. Introduction

Portfolio allocation is one of the most fundamental problems in quantitative finance. Traditional approaches such as mean-variance optimization, originally proposed by Harry Markowitz, focus on balancing expected return and variance. However, variance treats upside and downside volatility symmetrically and may not fully capture extreme losses during market crises.

In practice, investors care more about downside protection and drawdown management. Furthermore, market volatility is time-varying and often clusters during periods of financial stress.

This motivates our research question:

Can a volatility-informed portfolio optimization strategy under tail-risk constraints outperform traditional static allocation strategies?

To answer this question, we integrate volatility forecasting with tail-risk-aware portfolio optimization and evaluate its effectiveness through historical backtesting.
